Mars Atmospheric Feed

Mars' thin CO2 atmosphere is vital for future colonization. ISRU technologies, like Sabatier reactors, can convert atmospheric carbon dioxide into water and rocket fuel, enabling self-sufficiency for human missions.

Current ISS Location

The ISS orbits Earth at an altitude of approximately 420 kilometers (about 261 miles). It travels at a speed of 28,000 kilometers per hour (17,500 miles per hour), completing an orbit around the Earth roughly every 90 minutes.

Asteroid Tracking API

An asteroid tracking API provides real-time data on near-Earth objects. It's crucial for monitoring potential impacts, enabling early warning systems, and supporting space situational awareness, vital for planetary defense and future missions.

Space Data Visualization

Real-time Data

Real-time Data Streaming Visualization dynamically displays live space mission data. This includes continuously updating satellite telemetry, the ISS's precise location on a globe, or immediate Mars weather conditions, providing instantaneous operational insights.

Trajectory Visualization

Trajectory Visualization graphically plots the paths of space objects. It shows spacecraft orbits, asteroid flybys, or planetary trajectories in 2D or 3D, crucial for mission planning, navigation, and understanding celestial mechanics.
